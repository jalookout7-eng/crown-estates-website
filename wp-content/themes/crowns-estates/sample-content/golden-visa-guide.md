# Golden Visa Through Real Estate — A Step-by-Step Guide

Saudi Arabia's Premium Residency programme — widely referred to as the Saudi Golden Visa — is one of the most attractive residency-by-investment schemes in the Gulf. For UK investors buying property in the Kingdom, it offers a clear pathway to long-term legal status without the need for employment sponsorship.

## What Is the Premium Residency Programme?

Introduced in 2019 and expanded significantly since, the Saudi Premium Residency grants foreign nationals the right to live, work, and own property in the Kingdom on a long-term basis. It comes in two forms: a permanent residency option and a renewable one-year option. Both carry substantially more rights than a standard visitor or business visa.

## Minimum Investment Threshold

To qualify through real estate, applicants must invest a minimum of SAR 4 million (approximately £850,000 at current exchange rates) in eligible property. The investment must be in a designated freehold zone and the property must be registered in the applicant's name with a valid title deed issued by the Ministry of Justice.

## Eligible Property Types

Not all property qualifies. Eligible assets include completed freehold residential units and certain commercial properties within approved zones. Off-plan purchases may be considered once a title deed equivalent (such as an interim ownership certificate) is issued by the developer. Leasehold arrangements do not qualify.

## The Application Process

1. Identify and complete the qualifying property purchase with full title deed registration.
2. Prepare documentation: passport, title deed, proof of funds, health insurance, clean criminal record certificate.
3. Submit the application through the official Premium Residency Centre portal.
4. Attend biometric enrolment at a Saudi consulate or in-Kingdom centre.
5. Receive residency card, typically within 30 to 90 days of approval.

## Benefits of Holding Premium Residency

- Live and work in Saudi Arabia without an employer sponsor
- Open bank accounts and establish businesses in your own name
- Sponsor family members including spouse and dependent children
- Exit and re-enter the Kingdom freely
- Access to public services including healthcare and education
