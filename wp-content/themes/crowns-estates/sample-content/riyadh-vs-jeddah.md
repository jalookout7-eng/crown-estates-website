# Riyadh vs Jeddah — Where Should You Invest?

Saudi Arabia's two largest cities present distinct investment propositions. Riyadh is the Kingdom's political and commercial capital, driving economic activity at a national scale. Jeddah is the historic port city on the Red Sea, defined by its cosmopolitan character and coastal lifestyle. For investors, the choice between them comes down to strategy, risk appetite, and target tenant profile.

## Riyadh: The Business Capital

Riyadh is where government ministries, multinational headquarters, and the country's largest financial institutions are based. The city is growing rapidly, with a population expected to reach 15 to 20 million by 2030. Major infrastructure investments — including the Riyadh Metro, King Salman Park, and the New Murabba development — are reshaping the urban landscape and driving demand for quality housing.

The tenant base in Riyadh skews toward corporate professionals, government employees, and expatriate workers attached to multinational firms. This creates stable, long-term rental demand in well-located districts such as Olaya, Al Malqa, and the Diplomatic Quarter.

## Jeddah: The Coastal Lifestyle Market

Jeddah has long been the more liberal and outward-looking of Saudi Arabia's major cities. Its historic Al-Balad district is a UNESCO World Heritage site, and its corniche is one of the longest in the world. The city is a major hub for Hajj and Umrah pilgrimage logistics, generating significant hospitality and short-term rental demand.

Vision 2030 is investing heavily in Jeddah's tourism and entertainment infrastructure, including the AMAALA luxury resort development on the nearby Red Sea coast. This is attracting a new class of high-net-worth buyer looking for holiday homes and serviced apartment investments.

## Price Comparison

As of early 2026, prime residential prices in central Riyadh are running approximately 10 to 15% higher per square metre than comparable Jeddah locations. However, Jeddah's coastal premium properties — particularly waterfront units — command prices that rival Riyadh's top tier.

## Rental Yields

Both cities offer strong yields relative to UK benchmarks. Riyadh prime districts are yielding 7% to 9% gross on long-let residential. Jeddah short-term and serviced apartment assets are achieving 9% to 12% gross in well-managed schemes, reflecting the higher operational intensity of that model.

## Infrastructure Projects Driving Value

- Riyadh: Metro network now operational, King Salman Park (one of the world's largest urban parks), New Murabba mixed-use city centre
- Jeddah: Jeddah Central development (a 5.7 million sqm waterfront regeneration), Red Sea International Airport expansion, AMAALA resort connectivity

## Which City Is Right for You?

If you are seeking a stable, long-let residential investment with strong corporate tenant demand, Riyadh is the more straightforward choice. If you are comfortable with a slightly more operationally intensive model and want exposure to Saudi Arabia's growing tourism economy, Jeddah — and the broader Red Sea region — offers compelling upside.
