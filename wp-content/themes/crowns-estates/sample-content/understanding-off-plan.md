# Understanding Off-Plan Property in Saudi Arabia

Buying off-plan — that is, purchasing a property before it is built — has become one of the most common routes for investors entering the Saudi real estate market. The model offers attractive pricing and flexible payment structures, but it also requires careful due diligence. Here is what you need to understand before committing.

## What Is Off-Plan Property?

Off-plan property is sold by the developer before construction is complete, often at the point of planning approval or early build stage. Buyers typically receive a sales and purchase agreement, a payment schedule tied to construction milestones, and a projected handover date. The property does not yet have a full title deed — this is issued on completion.

## Payment Plans and How They Work

Saudi developers typically offer instalment-based payment plans spread over the construction period, with a final payment due on handover. A common structure might be 10% on reservation, 40% across construction milestones, and 50% on key handover. Some developers offer post-handover payment plans, allowing investors to start receiving rental income before the purchase is fully paid off.

## Developer Reliability

Not all developers are equal. The Saudi Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) maintains a register of licensed developers and requires escrow account management for off-plan sales. Buyers should verify that the developer is RERA-registered, that funds are held in escrow, and that the project has received the necessary planning approvals before committing any funds.

## Completion Timelines

Delays do occur, as they do in any market. Saudi off-plan projects typically carry projected handover dates of 18 to 36 months from launch. Reputable developers include contractual delay penalties in their SPAs. Investors should factor realistic timelines into their financial planning and not rely on rental income commencing before practical completion is confirmed.

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

- Buy only from RERA-licensed developers with a track record of delivery
- Confirm escrow account registration before transferring any funds
- Instruct an independent Saudi property lawyer to review the SPA
- Ensure the contract includes penalty clauses for delays and a clear exit mechanism
- Understand currency risk if your funds are held in sterling prior to drawdown
