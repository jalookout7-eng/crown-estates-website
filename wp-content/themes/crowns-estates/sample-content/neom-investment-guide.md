# NEOM Investment Guide — What's Available and What to Expect

NEOM is the most ambitious urban development project in human history. A $500 billion gigaproject on the Red Sea coast of northwestern Saudi Arabia, it is being built from scratch across 26,500 square kilometres. For investors, it represents both extraordinary potential and a genuinely novel set of considerations.

## What Is NEOM?

NEOM is a special economic zone being developed by the Saudi government under Vision 2030. It is designed to be a fully integrated region with its own governance, regulations, and economy — distinct from the rest of the Kingdom in meaningful ways. It encompasses multiple sub-projects, each targeting a different market segment and lifestyle proposition.

## The Line and Other Sub-Projects

The Line is NEOM's most publicised component: a linear city stretching 170 kilometres with no roads, no cars, and all amenities within a five-minute walk. Other sub-projects include Sindalah (a luxury yacht and island destination), Aqaba (a mountain tourism and adventure hub), and Oxagon (an advanced manufacturing port city). Each presents different investment profiles.

## Residential Options Currently Available

At this stage, direct residential sales to foreign investors are limited and handled through official NEOM channels and select appointed agents. Available inventory includes early-stage residential units in Sindalah and selected plots in NEOM's broader residential zones. Interest lists are open for The Line residential, with confirmed launches expected in phases from 2026 onward.

## Current Availability and Access

Most NEOM residential product is being sold through an expression-of-interest and invitation process rather than open-market listings. Working with an appointed agent who has direct NEOM developer relationships is currently the primary route to access.

## Expected Returns

Given the scale of government commitment and the unique nature of the asset, NEOM property is being positioned as a long-term capital appreciation play rather than an immediate yield investment. Early investors in comparable Gulf megaprojects have seen significant capital gains as delivery milestones are reached and the broader project gains credibility.
